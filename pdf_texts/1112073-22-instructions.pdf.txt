The 2 cm Flak 38 was a standard anti-aircraft gun of the
Wehrmacht and was used by Army, Waffen-SS, and Luft-
waffe AA units. Improvements were made to it in 1940, al-

though the earlier moder was stitin service througitout tie —
war. These improvements included the addition of a shield,
lighter weight, and better portability. The normal detach-
ment for each gun was'six men. It could be broken into six
loads for man-handling or carrying by horse over difficult
terrain. For action, the gun was emplaced on the ground,
but in an emergency could be fired from its two-wheel
trailer carriage. Barrel recoil and residual gas pressure
operated the automatic breech and firing action. The gun
had a multirole; AA, anti-tank, and anti-personnel. The Flak
38 in firing order weighed 924 Ibs, was 157 inches long
overall, had an emplaced height of 30 inches, a rate of fire of
220-450 rounds per minute, a maximum horizontal range of
5,246 yards, and maximum vertical range of 4,012 yards.
Elevation limits were —10 to +90 degrees. Ammunition
came in HE (high explosive) and AP (armour piercing)
forms and was loaded in 20 round magazines. The 2cm Flak
38 was in wide use by the Luftwaffe Flak companies defend-
ing airfields and other installations.
